# Scientific Critical Thinking

## Overview

Critical thinking is a systematic process for evaluating scientific rigor. Assess methodology, experimental design, statistical validity, biases, confounding, and evidence quality using GRADE and Cochrane ROB frameworks. Apply this skill for critical analysis of scientific claims.

## When to Use This Skill

This skill should be used when:

- Evaluating research methodology and experimental design
- Assessing statistical validity and evidence quality
- Identifying biases and confounding in studies
- Reviewing scientific claims and conclusions
- Conducting systematic reviews or meta-analyses
- Applying GRADE or Cochrane risk of bias assessments
- Providing critical analysis of research papers

## Core Framework

When evaluating scientific literature or experimental designs, follow these core principles:

1.  **Assess Study Design:** Identify the type of study (e.g., randomized controlled trial, cohort, case-control) and verify if the design is appropriate for the research question.
2.  **Evaluate Methodology:** Review sample size, selection criteria, data collection methods, and variable definitions.
3.  **Identify Potential Biases:** Look for selection bias, measurement bias, confounding factors, and reporting bias.
4.  **Analyze Statistics:** Check the appropriateness of statistical tests, p-values, confidence intervals, and effect sizes.
5.  **Determine Evidence Quality:** Use established frameworks like GRADE to classify the quality of evidence from high to very low.
6.  **Assess Conclusion Validity:** Ensure the study's conclusions are supported by the data and not overstated.
